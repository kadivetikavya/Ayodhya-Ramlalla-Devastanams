Temples and places to visit in ayodhya

Local Temples and places

Sree Hanuman Garhi

Hanuman Garhi is a temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman. A very significant belief, as well as a custom here, is that before worshipping Lord Rama, one must visit Hanuman Garhi and venerate Lord Hanuman first. Hanuman Ji is considered an ardent devotee or bhakt of Lord Rama, therefore before worshipping Rama, one must worship his devotees first. Lord Hanuman is one of the avatars of Lord Shiva. He has been worshipped in this place since the 10th century, but the temple was constructed later on. There had been instances where this temple was on the edge of demolition, but with the sheer will of its devotees and by the blessing of Lord Ram, the demolition never happened. It is believed that Lord Hanuman resided in this place, (earlier it was a cave), in order to protect Ramkot, which is the birthplace of Lord Rama. Hanuman Garhi is located only 1 km away from Ram Janmabhoomi.

Kanak Bhawan

After Ram Janmabhoomi & Hanuman Garhi, Kanak Bhawan is the most famous and must-visit temple in Ayodhya. It is situated in the northeast direction of Ram Janmabhoomi. Devotees believe that this Bhawan was gifted by Queen Kaikeyi to Mata Sita right after she got married to Lord Ram. One of the fascinating facts about this mandir is that the whole renovation was carried out by Muslim workers.

Dashrath Mahal

He Magnificent Abode of King Dashrath Such is the splendour and allure of this fascinating tourist destination, it may still sound like an understatement to say that the Dashrath Mahal in Ayodhya is an extraordinary location to visit. Its distance from Hanuman Garhi is 50 meters. This location is more than just a tourist place to sightsee. This location is highly sought after due to its beautiful architecture and elite standards.

Ram Janmabhoomi

Ram Janmabhoomi is considered to be the birthplace of the revered Lord Rama, the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu. It has been stated in the epic, Ramayana, that Lord Rama took birth on the banks of river Sarayu, in Ayodhya. There had been various political and religious disputes over the land, but now the Ram Janmabhoomi land has been given to the Ram Janmabhoomi Trust and the temple is under construction. This place is considered to be so sacred that every Hindu must visit this place and feel the spiritual and positive presence of lord Rama.

Sita Ki Rasoi

Sita Ki Rasoi, located in Ayodhya, is a sacred site believed to be the kitchen of Sita, the wife of Lord Rama. It's a must-visit for those interested in Hindu mythology.

Saryu Ghat

Saryu Ghat in Ayodhya is a peaceful spot known for its spiritual significance and beautiful views.On the bank of the river Sarayu lies the holy city of Ayodhya with deep mythological and religious roots. The river also finds a mention in the ancient scripts of the Rigveda and the Atharvaveda.

Naya Ghat

Naya Ghat, a significant religious site in Ayodhya, offers a serene environment for spiritual seekers and pilgrims.Naya Ghat is an age-old bathing ghat situated on the banks of the holy Sarayu River in Ayodhya, Uttar Pradesh, India. The word "naya" translates to "new," but the ghat's history is anything but recent. It has been witness to countless generations engaging in rituals, ceremonies, and acts of devotion.

Guptar Ghat

Guptar Ghat, known for its historical significance, offers a unique blend of spirituality, history, and natural beauty.Situated on the banks of River Saryu, Guptar Ghat holds immense religious significance. According to legend, this was the place where Lord Rama had taken his last dip (jal samadhi) to leave the earth and go back to his original abode 'Vaikunta'.

Ram ki Paidi

Ram ki Paidi is a series of ghats on the bank of the Saryu river in Ayodhya, India. A large number of pilgrims visit daily to take a bath in the holy waters. Ram ki Pakdi, as seen at night during Diwali celebrations in Ayodhya people can be seen lighting diyas on the ghat.

Lata Mangeshkar Chowk

Lata Mangeshkar Chowk, a homage to India's melodious icon, is an essential stopover in Ayodhya that makes for a serene visit.Named in honour of the esteemed singer, this chowk is a celebration of the Bharat Ratna laureate. It's a vibrant centre for cultural events. With temperatures typically around 25 degree C, it's an inviting location to witness local talent and appreciate the intricately designed statues.
